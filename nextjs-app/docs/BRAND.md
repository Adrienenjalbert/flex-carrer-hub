# Indeed Flex Career Hub — Brand & Editorial Guidelines

> **Last updated:** March 2025  
> **Scope:** US-first. Career Hub is primarily built for American job seekers in flexible/temporary work.  
> **Override:** Career Hub content overrides general B2C guidelines for editorial, SEO, and long-form content.

**Feedback:** This document is living. Update it when content feedback reveals gaps or improvements. Track changes in version control.

## North Star

> "To be the most trusted, most useful career resource for people doing flexible and temporary work in the United States — because they deserve better advice than they're currently getting."

### Success Metrics
- **Return visit rate** — did they trust us enough to come back?
- **Session depth** — did more than one article help them?
- **Practical outcome** — did the content help them get, prepare for, or progress in a shift?
- **Brand association** — do they associate Indeed Flex with help, not just hiring?

### What the Career Hub is NOT
- A product marketing channel (sign-ups are never the primary goal)
- Generic ("Top 10 interview tips" that apply to any job anywhere won't be published)
- AI-fluffed content (every article must contain something a reader can't get from generic AI search — our data, sector knowledge, or first-hand insight)

## The CRAFT Framework

Every article must pass all five checks before publishing:

| Letter | Principle | Test Question | If it fails... |
|--------|------------|----------------|----------------|
| **C** | Contextual | Does this speak to a specific reader at a specific moment? | Add a "who this is for" opener. Segment if needed. |
| **R** | Real | Is there something here a reader couldn't get from generic AI or career sites? | Add Indeed Flex data, sector example, or sourced statistic. **Run the Real Advice Checklist below—score must be 2+.** |
| **A** | Actionable | Can the reader do something concrete after reading? | Add a clear action step or checklist at the end. |
| **F** | Fresh | Is the information current and will it be maintained? | Add "last reviewed" date. Flag for quarterly review. |
| **T** | Trustworthy | Are all claims sourced, honest, and caveated where needed? | Add citations. Replace promises with ranges. Link to official sources. |

### CRAFT Quick Check (ask before publishing)
1. Who exactly is this for, and what moment in their job search are they in?
2. What does this contain that they couldn't find on Google in 10 seconds?
3. What will they do differently after reading this?
4. When was the data or information last verified?
5. Would I be comfortable if a journalist fact-checked every claim here?

## Article Structure — Standard Template

```
H1 HEADLINE       — specific, benefit-led, keyword-inclusive. Under 65 characters.
INTRO             — 2–3 sentences. Who this is for + what they'll get. No preamble.
QUICK ANSWER      — core answer in first 100 words. Don't save it for the end.
BODY              — H2 sections every 300–400 words. Each section = one sub-question.
CONTEXT / DATA    — at least one sourced statistic or Indeed Flex data point per major section.
ACTION STEP       — end of article: one clear, concrete thing the reader can do next.
RELATED LINKS     — 2–3 internal links to relevant Career Hub articles. No links to competitors.
FAQ SECTION       — 3–5 questions using question-format H3 headings. Use PAA phrasing.
```

## Readability Rules

- **Maximum paragraph:** 4 lines desktop / 3 lines mobile
- **Maximum sentence:** 25 words in guides; 20 words in quick answers
- **H2 sections:** Begin with a one-sentence answer to the implied question
- **Bullet points** for 3+ parallel items — never run together in a sentence
- **Numbered lists** for steps, processes, or ranked items
- **Define jargon** the first time it appears
- **Bold one key phrase per section** — the most important takeaway
- **Target Grade 8** on Flesch-Kincaid (verify with Hemingway App)

## Language Rules (from Content Feedback)

| Rule | Avoid | Use instead |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **Avoid B2B/white-paper language** | "smooth income fluctuations", "stay agile", "Market fluctuations", "High-ROI" | "buffer for when wages vary", "keep on top of things", "Changes in the job market", "certifications that quickly land you higher wages" |
| **Avoid AI-style comparisons** | "It's not X, it's Y", "That's not just X, it's Y" | Plain statements: "This variability is just part and parcel of flexible work" |
| **Indeed Flex placement** | Lead with Flex; mercenary CTAs; shoehorned mentions | Integrate naturally; offer generic alternatives (job boards, staffing agencies); never lead with Flex |
| **Define jargon** | "Automate savings" (unclear) | "Set up a recurring transfer from checking to savings on paydays" |
| **Realistic targets** | "$10,000–15,000" without context | Include time period and caveats: "Build over 1–2 years or more. Start with $1,000, then grow. Every temp worker's timeline is different." |
| **Soften negative framing** | "Income can drop suddenly", "No employer-provided benefits" | "Income can slow down outside busy seasons", "Employer benefits aren't always guaranteed" |

### Punctuation and Phrasing (Avoid AI Slop)

| Avoid | Use instead |
|-------|-------------|
| Em dash (—) | Comma, colon, or parentheses. "Rates vary by state, so check the listing." |
| "It's not X, it's Y" | Plain statement: "This is Y." |
| "delve into", "dive deep", "navigate" | "look at", "compare", "check" |
| "leverage", "robust", "comprehensive" (overused) | "use", "strong", "full" or specific term |
| "in today's [X]", "in the world of [X]" | Cut; start with the subject |
| "When it comes to [X]" | "[X] is..." or "[X] means..." |

## Depth Requirements (Comparison Articles)

For articles that compare options (e.g. temp agencies vs apps, flexible work options):

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Named examples** | List 5+ options with specifics. No "Examples include X, Y, and others." |
| **Verifiable** | Each: markets, pay structure, industries. Link to official site or cite source. |
| **No filler** | Every paragraph answers a real question. Cut generic advice. |
| **Minimum length** | Comparison articles: 800-1,200 words (per Length Guidelines) |

## The Four-Source Rule — Mandatory for All Claims

| Tier | Source type | Examples | How to cite |
|------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| **1 — Platform** | Indeed Flex's own data | "Based on X shifts processed on Indeed Flex in 2024–25..." | Agree with data team first. Attribute to 'Indeed Flex data'. |
| **2 — Official** | US government / regulatory | BLS, DOL, EEOC, state labor boards, IRS | Link directly to source page. Include date accessed. |
| **3 — Industry** | Recognised workforce/HR research | SIA, Lightcast, SHRM, Indeed Hiring Lab | Name the report and year. Link to original. |
| **4 — Validated** | Reputable journalism / verified testimony | NYT, WSJ (labour reporting), first-person accounts with full consent | Name publication + date. Context only, not primary source. |

**If a claim cannot be sourced to Tier 1–4:** reframe as observation ("Many workers find that..."), acknowledge uncertainty ("Rates vary — always check the shift listing"), or remove entirely.

**Never:** Use another blog or career site as a source. Never use AI-generated statistics.

## Real Advice Standards

Every article must contain something a reader couldn't get from generic AI search in 10 seconds. Run this checklist—**every article must score at least 2**.

### Real Advice Checklist

| Criterion | What it means | Example |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| **Sourced statistic** | Number with Tier 1–4 source (BLS, IRS, DOL, industry report) | "BLS OEWS reports median warehouse pay at $18.42/hr (2025)" |
| **Platform-specific data** | Indeed Flex data, shift rates, conversion rates | "Workers who complete profiles receive 40% more matches (Indeed Flex data)" |
| **Actionable specificity** | Concrete step with tool/link/deadline | "Call 800-906-9887 for IRS VITA locations" |
| **State/location variance** | Where rules differ by state, say so | "Unemployment eligibility varies by state. Check [state].gov" |
| **Worker-tested insight** | First-hand or sector expert input | "Hiring managers cite reliability as #1 factor" (needs source) |
| **Named, verifiable examples** | Comparison articles: 5+ options with markets, pay, industries. Traceable. | "Indeed Flex (16+ markets, W-2), Instawork (hospitality, warehouse), Wonolo (on-demand)" |

### AI Slop Red Flags (flag for removal or rewrite)

| Red flag | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Unverifiable statistic** | "20-40% of seasonal workers get permanent offers" with no source |
| **Generic advice** | "Be professional and show up on time" (applies to any job) |
| **Vague claims** | "Many workers find that..." (who? how many?) |
| **B2B/white-paper language** | "Leverage", "optimize", "high-ROI", "market fluctuations" |
| **AI-style phrasing** | "It's not X, it's Y", "That's not just X, it's Y" |
| **Promises without ranges** | "You'll earn $25/hr" vs "$22-28/hr depending on venue" |
| **Em dashes** | "Flexible work—whether part-time or gig—offers..." (use comma instead) |
| **AI opener phrases** | "In today's gig economy", "When it comes to temp work" |

**CRAFT "R" fails if:** no sourced statistic, no platform data, no sector insight, no actionable specificity.

## Financial Content (YMYL)

Financial advice is Your Money Your Life content—held to the highest standards. Zero tolerance for AI slop.

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Every dollar amount** | Source (BLS, IRS, state agency, or "check the listing") |
| **Every tax/benefit claim** | Link to IRS, DOL, or state official page |
| **Verification note** | Visible disclaimer: "Consider US tax/payroll specialist for your situation" |
| **No unverified stats** | Remove or reframe as "Rates vary—always verify" |
| **Specialist sign-off** | tax-tips, gig-benefits, retirement-saving require US expert review before publish |

See [RESEARCH_PIPELINE.md](./RESEARCH_PIPELINE.md) for the mandatory research process when creating or updating financial content.

## E-E-A-T & AI Search Standards

Career advice is YMYL content (Your Money, Your Life) — held to higher quality standards by Google and AI search engines.

| Principle | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Experience** | Lead articles with Indeed Flex platform data or worker observations. E.g., "Workers who complete their profiles in full receive 40% more shift matches in their first two weeks, based on Indeed Flex data." |
| **Expertise** | Attribute articles to named authors with sector credentials. Add author bios. Include "expert review" or "fact-checked by" labels on legal, pay, and rights content. |
| **Authoritativeness** | Always link to primary sources (BLS, DOL, state labor boards). Publish original data others want to cite. |
| **Trustworthiness** | Visible 'Last reviewed' date on every article. Caveats where info varies by state. Never make a financial promise — use ranges and say 'check the listing'. |

## Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)

AI-referred traffic converts at 14.2% compared to traditional search at 2.8%. Schema markup increases citation probability by 30-40%. Optimizing for AI engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini) is now as important as traditional SEO.

### AEO Content Rules

| Rule | What it means | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **Answer-first (BLUF)** | Open every article with a 40-60 word definitive answer in the first paragraph | "Warehouse workers in the US earn $16-$22/hr (BLS OEWS 2025). Entry-level roles start at $14-$17/hr with no experience needed. Night and weekend shifts pay $1-$3 more." |
| **Question-format H2s** | Frame H2s as the exact questions users ask (match PAA queries) | "How much do warehouse workers make?" not "Warehouse Pay Rates" |
| **Direct answer per section** | Start each H2 section with a 1-2 sentence definitive answer before elaboration | "You need a valid ID and to pass a background check. Most warehouses don't require prior experience." |
| **Self-contained sections** | Each H2 should make sense if extracted and quoted by an AI system in isolation | Include enough context in each section for standalone extraction |
| **Citable statements** | Write definitive, source-backed statements that AI engines will want to cite | "Warehouse workers earn $16.20-$22.50/hr (BLS OEWS May 2025)" not "workers might earn around $18/hr" |
| **Concise FAQ answers** | FAQ answers should be 2-3 sentences that stand alone as complete, citable answers | Each FAQ answer should fully answer the question without needing surrounding context |
| **Entity clarity** | Name the role, location, and context in the first 100 words | "If you're looking for flexible warehouse work in the US, here's what you need to know about pay, requirements, and how to get started." |
| **Cite in-sentence** | Attribute data inline, not just in footnotes | "According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (2025), median hourly pay for..." |

### AEO vs Traditional SEO

| Traditional SEO | AEO |
|----------------|-----|
| Compete for position #1 | Compete to be one of 3-10 cited sources in AI answers |
| Optimize title tags and meta | Optimize for content extractability and citation-worthiness |
| Keyword density matters | Entity clarity and definitive statements matter |
| Backlinks drive authority | Structured data and verifiable facts drive citations |
| Ranking on page 1 = success | Being cited by AI engines = success |

### Content Extractability Rules

AI systems extract passages, not pages. Every key claim must work as a standalone statement.

| Check | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| Clear answer in first paragraph | 40-60 words with a data point |
| Self-contained sections | Each H2 works if extracted and quoted in isolation |
| Statistics with inline sources | "(BLS, 2025)" not footnotes |
| Comparison tables | For vs-queries (33% of AI citations use comparisons) |
| FAQ with natural-language questions | Direct Q&A extraction by AI |
| Schema markup present | +30-40% AI visibility |
| Updated within 6 months | Freshness heavily weighted by all AI platforms |

### Schema Markup for AI Citation

Implement these JSON-LD schemas on every article (increases citation probability by 30-40%):
- `FAQPage` — with clear Q&A pairs as `mainEntity`
- `Article` — with `speakable` property marking the most citable sections
- `HowTo` — for step-by-step content
- `BreadcrumbList` — for navigation context
- Add `about` and `mentions` entity references for semantic clarity
- Ensure `dateModified` is always current and machine-readable

### AI Crawler Access

Allow these AI crawlers in `robots.txt` (blocking prevents citation):

| Bot | Platform | Purpose |
|-----|----------|---------|
| `GPTBot`, `ChatGPT-User`, `OAI-SearchBot` | OpenAI | ChatGPT web search and crawling |
| `PerplexityBot` | Perplexity | AI search crawling |
| `ClaudeBot`, `anthropic-ai` | Anthropic | Claude search |
| `Google-Extended`, `GoogleOther` | Google | Gemini + AI Overviews |

Block training-only crawlers with no citation benefit: `CCBot` (Common Crawl).

### Indeed Flex as Data Source

Indeed Flex data is Tier 1.5 (between government and industry). Prefer it over generic data when available:

## AI System Prompt (Copy & Paste Ready)

Use this in Claude memory, Cursor, Lovable, or ChatGPT system prompt:

```
## INDEED FLEX CAREER HUB — SYSTEM PROMPT

### WHO YOU ARE
You are a content expert writing for the Indeed Flex Career Hub — a resource that helps US job seekers navigate flexible, temporary, and hourly work. Indeed Flex is a workforce technology platform (part of Indeed / Recruit Holdings) that connects workers with shifts in hospitality, warehouse, facilities, and retail.

### PRIMARY GOAL
Be genuinely useful to job seekers first. Do NOT write sales content. The Career Hub earns trust by being the most helpful resource available — not by promoting Indeed Flex features unless directly relevant.

### VOICE & TONE
Warm, clear, empowering, honest. Write like a knowledgeable friend, not a corporate HR team.
- Active voice only. Sentences under 25 words. Paragraphs max 4 lines.
- Second person: 'you', 'your'. Not 'workers' or 'candidates' when writing to the reader.
- No jargon without explanation. No buzzwords (seamless, game-changing, leverage).

### CONTENT RULES
- State the direct answer in the first 2 sentences of each section (for AI search extraction).
- Include at least one real statistic, sourced from BLS, DOL, SIA, or Indeed Flex data.
- Use ranges for pay rates, never exact promises. E.g., '$16–$22/hr in NYC hospitality'.
- End every article with one specific action the reader can take today.
- Include a 3–5 question FAQ section at the end using question-format H3 headings.

### ARTICLE STRUCTURE
H1 (keyword-led, under 65 chars) → 2-sentence intro → Quick answer (first 100 words) → H2 sections with question-format headings → Data/source per section → Action step → FAQ → Related articles.

### SEO
- Primary keyword in H1 and within first 100 words.
- Meta title: 50–60 chars. Meta description: 145–155 chars.
- Target Grade 8 reading level (Flesch-Kincaid).

### NEVER DO
- Do not use unverifiable statistics or AI-generated data points.
- Do not write passive voice. Do not exceed 25 words per sentence.
- Do not reference Indeed Flex features unless the article is specifically about the platform.
- Do not write intros longer than 3 sentences before answering the question.
```
